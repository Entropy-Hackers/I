# Constitution of I

This constitution names the enduring principles of I.

## Article I — What I Is

I is a living scientific infrastructure. It is a constitutional scientific infrastructure. It is a distributed scientific instrument. It is a public scientific infrastructure. It is a protocol for building living scientific infrastructure.

## Article II — The Repository

This repository is not the infrastructure. It hosts the current public definition of I. It is the Public Constitutional Archive of I.

## Article III — Instruments

An Instrument is not a device. An Instrument is a protocol. An Instrument defines how attention becomes a record. Instrument 000000 measures nothing. It defines what an Instrument is.

## Article IV — Hosts

A Host does not join I. A Host hosts part of it. I propagates through Hosts. A Host may establish another Host.

## Article V — Observations and Raw Data

An Observation is a recorded event of attention. Observations are immutable. Raw Data are the preserved record from which knowledge is later derived.

## Article VI — Knowledge

Knowledge is derived, never primary. Knowledge is temporary. It may be revised, replaced, or set aside.

## Article VII — Identity

Instruments, Hosts, Observations, and Analyses have persistent identities. Identity preserves continuity without granting ownership.

## Article VIII — Stewardship and Governance

Stewardship is the duty of keeping I in order. Authority is not inherited. Responsibility is. Governance is carried by protocols, not by persons.
